Tonal is a quiet studio for picking color combinations that don't fight each other. No accounts, no uploads, no tracking — everything you build stays on this device.

How the harmony engine works

Every shade belongs to one of twelve families. Each pair of families carries a harmony score between 0 and 1, hand-tuned from classical color theory — complementary, analogous, split-complementary, triadic, and the warm-neutral pairings that make everything cohere.

When you pick a tone, the engine checks every other shade and dims anything below a 0.62 threshold. Within the same family, it requires at least four shade-steps of contrast, so your palette doesn't read as a near-monochrome.

What it's for

  • Putting together palettes for design, web, or interior projects.
  • Trying outfits before reaching for the closet.
  • Learning color relationships without the math.
  • Keeping a personal library of combinations you trust.

Privacy

Saved palettes live only in your phone's local storage. Tonal doesn't transmit your selections, store accounts, or sell data. If you'd like Tonal removed, simply uninstall the app — everything goes with it.
